c. 2560 BC
The Great Pyramid of Giza is constructed by Pharaoh Khufu (Cheops).

c. 2510 BC
Pyramid of Menkaure is built at Giza.

605 BC - 562 BC
The Hanging Gardens of Babylon, according to tradition, are built by Nebuchadnezzar II.

c. 550 BC - c. 430 BC
Construction of the Temple of Artemis at Ephesus, one of the Seven Wonders 	of the Ancient World.

c. 430 BC
The cult statue of Zeus by Phidias is dedicated in the Temple of Zeus, 	Olympia. It is one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.

c. 367 BC - 350 BC
The Mausoleum at Halicarnassus is built, tomb of Mausolus, ruler of Caria.

21 Jul 356 BC
The Temple of Artemis at Ephesus is destroyed by a deliberate fire.

c. 355 BC - c. 330 BC
The Temple of Artemis at Ephesus is rebuilt/restored.

c. 300 BC - c. 280 BC
The Lighthouse of Alexandria is built, one of the Seven Wonders of the 	Ancient World.

c. 292 BC - c. 280 BC
The Colossus of Rhodes, a representation of Helios, is built in Rhodes town harbour, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.

228 BC - 226 BC
The Colossus of Rhodes is toppled by an earthquake.

225 BC
Philo of Byzantium records The Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.

c. 267 AD
The Goths plunder and destroy the Temple of Artemis at Ephesus.

395 AD
The statue of Zeus at Olympia is removed to Constantinople.

401 AD
The Temple of Artemis at Ephesus is destroyed by a Christian mob.

475 AD
According to the Byzantine historian Zonaras, the statue of Zeus from 	Olympia is destroyed by a fire in Constantinople.

c. 654 AD
According to Theophanes, a Jewish merchant transports the pieces of the fallen Colossus of Rhodes to Edessa for melting down.

956 AD
The Lighthouse of Alexandria partially collapses following earthquake damage sustained in 950 AD.

c. 1100 AD
A domed mosque is added to the repaired Lighthouse of Alexandria.

c. 1330 AD
The Lighthouse of Alexandria definitively collapses, probably due to an earthquake.

c. 1494 AD
The Knights of Saint John of Malta use parts of the Mausoleum of 		Halicarnassus in the walls of the castle of Saint Peter at Bodrum.
