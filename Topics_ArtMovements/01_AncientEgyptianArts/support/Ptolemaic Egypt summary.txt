Summary

Historical Context: Ptolemaic Egypt existed from 323 to 30 BC, ruled by the Macedonian Ptolemaic dynasty after Alexander the Great's conquest.

Cultural Integration: Greek immigrants introduced new language, religion, and culture, with Alexandria becoming a major Hellenistic city.

Governance: The Ptolemies maintained Egypt's traditional administrative structure but introduced Greek fiscal institutions and legal systems.

Economic and Social Changes: The economy was modernized with coinage, and Greek culture influenced urban life, while rural areas remained more traditional.
