- **Greek Bronze Age**: Also known as the Aegean Civilization, centered around the Aegean Sea.
- **Primary Locations**:
  - **Cyclades**: Islands southeast of mainland Greece.
  - **Crete**: South of mainland Greece.
  - **Greek Mainland**.

- **Civilizations**:
  - **Cycladic Civilization** (c. 3300 - 2000 BC): Known for marble female figurines with large oval faces and elongated noses. Main sites: Keros, Grotta, Phylakopi, Syros.
  - **Minoan Civilization** (c. 2700 - 1100 BC): Located in Crete. Known for frescoes, pottery, and advanced trade and writing systems. Main sites: Knossos, Malia, Phaistos, Zakros.
  - **Mycenaean Civilization** (c. 3200 - 1050 BC): Located on mainland Greece. Known for strong warrior culture and artworks influenced by Minoans. Main sites: Mycenae, Athens, Thebes, Pylos, Sparta.

- **Cultural Developments**:
  - **Writing Systems**: Linear A and Linear B.
  - **Trade**: Increased activities and exchange of goods like gold, glass, copper, and ivory.
  - **Tools**: Development of various new tools.

- **Art and Architecture**:
  - **Cycladic Art**: Marble female figurines.
  - **Minoan Art**: Frescoes featuring animals and natural landscapes, pottery (amphora, beakers, pithos, rhyta), gold jewelry, sculptures, and multi-story palaces.
  - **Mycenaean Art**: Frescoes depicting battles, animals, and nature; geometric and formal style.

- **Significant Sites and Artifacts**:
  - **Lion Gate** (c. 1250 BC): Architectural relief sculpture at the main entrance to the Acropolis in Mycenae, depicting two lions or lionesses.
